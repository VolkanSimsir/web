AI Projects

AutoInference: Multiple inference options in a single library

The AutoInference library I developed combines different inference methods for LLMs such as Transformers, Unsloth, vLLM, and llama.cpp into a single unified library, enabling flexible and high-performance inference workflows. It also supports LLM quantization techniques such as AWQ, GPTQ, and bitsandbytes, offering optimized and low-memory inference based on user defined parameters.

Fine-Tuning, Optimization, Quantization, and Inference of LLMs

I perform fine-tuning on large language models (LLMs) such as LLama, Qwen, Gemma, and Deepseek. In this process, I apply techniques like SFT, GRPO, RLHF, and RL. I manage the entire workflow, including hyperparameter optimization for different models and datasets, as well as data collection, cleaning, structuring, and deploying the models locally. During the inference phase, I utilize tools such as vLLM and llama.cpp, and I perform quantization in GGUF format to ensure efficient model deployment. Tools/Formats Used: Transformers, Unsloth, llama.cpp, vLLM, GGUF
